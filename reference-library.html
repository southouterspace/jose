Worked Example · Framing a Wall with a Door or Window opening-framing

The opening is where four reference layers meet on one detail: the geometry of king/jack/cripple studs, the span of the header, the building-science penalty of an oversized header, and the fasteners that tie it together. Resolved in order:

Geometry Thallon — Graphic Guide to Frame Construction Opening framing King studs, jack/trimmer studs, cripples above/below, sill & header backing — the diagrammable conventions your member generator places around any opening in a wall line.
Sizing IRC — prescriptive header tables R602.7Tables R602.7(1–3) Default header size by span, supported load (roof/floor/wall above), and species/grade. Lookup first — only reach for NDS/Ambrose if the opening exceeds the prescriptive envelope.
Building science Lstiburek — Builder's Guides Insulated / right-sized headers An oversized solid header is a thermal short-circuit. Advanced-framing detailing (single-member + insulation, or sized-to-load) reduces the framing factor your whole-wall-R calc consumes.
Fasteners IRC — Fastening Schedule Table R602.3(1) Header-to-king, jack-to-king, cripple-to-plate connections each resolve to a count keyed on connection type & method (end-nail vs toe-nail). The connection graph sums to the nail.

For machine reference

Every card carries data-id, data-role, data-layer, and data-topics. The canonical structured copy — books, roles, section anchors, edition cautions, and a subjectIndex mapping each modeling subject to its references — lives in the JSON block below under #library-data. To resolve a problem: match the subject to subjectIndex, follow the bookId references, read each book's anchors and editionCaution.

Section numbers (e.g. R602.7) follow common I-code usage but shift between cycles — treat them as pointers to verify against the edition your jurisdiction adopted, not constants.
